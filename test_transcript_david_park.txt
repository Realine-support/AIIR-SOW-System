AIIR Consulting Discovery Call Transcript

Duration: 45 minutes

Participants:
- Sarah Chen, VP of Operations at TechVenture Inc

Michael Rodriguez: Good morning, Sarah. Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I understand you're interested in our executive coaching services for one of your senior leaders.

Michael Rodriguez: Tell me more about that.

Sarah Chen: Well, David is extremely technical and results-driven. He gets things done. But as we've grown, we're noticing that his leadership style hasn't evolved with the organization. He tends to be very directive, sometimes micromanages his senior engineers, and there's feedback that he doesn't always create space for others to contribute ideas. We've had two senior engineering managers leave in the past six months, and in their exit interviews, they mentioned feeling stifled.

Michael Rodriguez: That's challenging. How does David see the situation?

Sarah Chen: That's the thing - he knows something isn't working. He came to me last month and said, "Sarah, I know I need to lead differently, but I don't know how." He's incredibly self-aware about his technical strengths but admits he's never had formal leadership development. He grew up as an individual contributor and got promoted based on technical excellence.

Michael Rodriguez: That self-awareness is actually a great sign. What outcomes are you hoping for?

Sarah Chen: We want David to become a more strategic, empowering leader. Someone who can develop other leaders, delegate effectively, and create a culture of innovation rather than just execution. We're planning to expand internationally next year, and we need him to be able to lead at that scale.

Michael Rodriguez: And what's David's perspective? Is he committed to this development?

Sarah Chen: Absolutely. In fact, he's eager. He told me, "I want to be the leader this team deserves, not just the manager they have." He's open to feedback, willing to be vulnerable, and ready to do the work. He just needs the right support structure.

Michael Rodriguez: That's excellent. Let me ask about your culture and expectations. How would you describe your leadership philosophy at TechVenture?

Sarah Chen: We're a fast-paced startup environment, but we really value psychological safety and innovation. Our CEO, Jennifer Wu, is big on servant leadership. We want leaders who can balance driving results with developing people. We're not looking for command-and-control - we want coaches and mentors.

Michael Rodriguez: Perfect. And in terms of David's role - who does he report to?

Sarah Chen: He reports directly to me. I'm his main point of contact, but he also works very closely with our CTO, Lisa Martinez, and our Chief Product Officer. He manages five senior engineering directors.

Michael Rodriguez: How would success look for this engagement? If we're sitting here in nine months, what would need to be true for you to say this was worth the investment?

Sarah Chen: Great question. First, I'd want to see David delegating more effectively and trusting his leadership team. Second, better retention - we can't afford to lose more senior talent. Third, I'd want feedback from his team that they feel more empowered and that David is creating space for innovation. And finally, David himself should feel more confident and less stressed. He's been burning out trying to do everything himself.

Michael Rodriguez: Those are clear, measurable outcomes. Now, let's talk about logistics. What's your timeline?

Sarah Chen: We'd like to start within the next month if possible. We have a big product launch in Q3, and I think having coaching support during that period would be valuable.

Michael Rodriguez: And budget-wise, what are you working with?

Sarah Chen: We've allocated up to $50,000 for executive coaching this fiscal year. We see this as a critical investment in our leadership bench.

Michael Rodriguez: That works well. Based on what you've described, I'd recommend our IGNITE program. It's specifically designed for high-potential leaders who need to make the transition from tactical execution to strategic leadership. Sarah Chen: That sounds perfect. What does the assessment process look like?

Michael Rodriguez: We'd start with stakeholder interviews - likely with you, Lisa, the CPO, and several of David's direct reports and peers. Then David would complete four validated assessment instruments that measure personality, leadership competencies, and potential derailers. Sarah Chen: I like that it's data-driven. David responds well to concrete information. How often would he meet with his coach?

Michael Rodriguez: Typically, we do 90-minute sessions every two to three weeks. That gives David time to practice new behaviors between sessions. We also build in three stakeholder meetings throughout the engagement - one at the beginning to align on goals, one at the midpoint to check progress, and one at the end to celebrate wins and plan for ongoing development.

Sarah Chen: And you mentioned nine months?

Michael Rodriguez: Yes, the IGNITE program runs six to nine months depending on scheduling. Nine months gives us enough runway to see real behavioral change and leadership growth.

Sarah Chen: This all sounds great. What are the next steps?

Michael Rodriguez: I'll send you a detailed proposal by end of week. It'll include the program structure, timeline, investment details, and our recommended coach for David. Michael Rodriguez: Wonderful. One last question - does David know you're exploring coaching for him?

Sarah Chen: Yes, absolutely. We talked about it together. He's excited and actually asked me to make this call. He said, "I don't want to be the reason we can't scale."
